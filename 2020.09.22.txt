30, TITLE:      Latin BERT: A Contextual Language Model for Classical Philology
http://arxiv.org/abs/2009.10053
AUTHORS:        David Bamman ; Patrick J. Burns
CATEGORY:       cs.CL [cs.CL]
HIGHLIGHT:      We present Latin BERT, a contextual language model for the Latin language, trained on 642.7 million words from a variety of sources spanning the Classical era to the 21st century. In a series of case studies, we illustrate the affordances of this language-specific model both for work in natural language processing for Latin and in using computational methods for traditional scholarship: we show that Latin BERT achieves a new state of the art for part-of-speech tagging on all three Universal Dependency datasets for Latin and can be used for predicting missing text (including critical emendations); we create a new dataset for assessing word sense disambiguation for Latin and demonstrate that Latin BERT outperforms static word embeddings; and we show that it can be used for semantically-informed search by querying contextual nearest neighbors.
